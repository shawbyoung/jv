Shaw Young

I spent some terrible winters in Ann Arbor getting my bachelor's (after spending my adolescence in the Florida Keys, San Diego, and Virginia) but met some good people and now am based out of New York City.

It'll be ~4 years until I've spent more time programming than not and am most proud of my (humble) work on LLVM. I've worked at a few tech companies with varying levels of enthusiasm.

I don't believe in the AI hype (transformers and their tooling cannot replicate human intelligence) but by God do investors love to see "AI" in earnings reports and pitch decks. I think the popping of the AI bubble will accompany the next great financial crisis. I think Yann LeCun is right and true AGI/superintelligence/whatever requires a world model. I think LLMs will (continue to) be great ways to query existing knowledge but nothing more. I think AI will ultimately create more good than bad (the automation of labor) but severely negatively impact the quality of life for the average person (the automation of labor) in the short term.

I worry about the global economy, climate, microplastics, and short form content.
